Litreture Survey

The integration of advanced technologies in educational contexts has facilitated significant advancements in supporting diverse student needs. Baker et al. [1] discuss the implementation of real-time sentiment analysis and behavioral monitoring systems to provide timely emotional support within educational environments. This approach allows educators to detect signs of emotional distress promptly and intervene effectively, promoting student well-being and academic success. Smith et al. [2] highlight the development of deep learning-based systems for real-time translation of sign language, enhancing communication accessibility for hearing impaired individuals in educational settings. Lee et al. [3] explore the application of computer vision technologies to improve navigation and social interaction among visually impaired students, demonstrating significant strides in promoting independence and engagement. Jones et al. [4] review interactive digital platforms designed to facilitate sign language learning, offering personalized learning experiences that empower hearing impaired individuals to develop effective communication skills. These studies collectively illustrate how technological innovations are reshaping educational practices to create more inclusive and supportive learning environments for all students.

In today's educational landscape, the integration of technology into learning environments has become increasingly prevalent, marking a shift towards E-learning platforms. These platforms utilize digital tools and resources such as videos, interactive modules, and online assessments to deliver educational content remotely.
While E-learning offers flexibility and convenience, it also presents unique challenges, particularly in addressing the diverse needs of students. Key areas of focus include enhancing accessibility for individuals with disabilities, such as hearing impaired and visually impaired students, and promoting inclusive learning experiences through innovative technologies like, emotional wellbeing support,sign language communication, object detection with facial expression recognition and sign language learning. By leveraging these advancements, E-learning endeavors to provide a more equitable and supportive educational environment for all learners, regardless of their abilities or geographical location.
The research gap in assistive technology lies in the lack of a comprehensive system addressing four critical components. Firstly, there is a need for better systems to identify and support stressed individuals who are hearing or visually impaired, as current platforms lack effective intervention mechanisms. Secondly, there is a notable gap in object detection technologies specifically designed to help visually impaired students recognize and navigate around objects. Thirdly, existing sign language communication tools are insufficient and require more advanced real-time translation systems between sign language and spoken or written language to improve communication for hearing and visually impaired individuals. Lastly, sign language learning systems are underdeveloped, needing tools that can verify signs and provide assessments for learning purposes. Addressing these gaps would create a more holistic and user-centered support system for hearing and visually impaired individuals, as well as for caregivers, teachers, and other stakeholders.

  1. Stress Detection and Emotional Well-being Support:
    In today's educational environment, students, including those with hearing impairments, face unprecedented challenges that can impact their emotional well-being. Implementing effective stress detection and emotional support systems is crucial to ensure their mental health needs are addressed promptly and comprehensively. Current approaches often lack tailored solutions for identifying and supporting students experiencing stress or emotional distress.

  2. Sign Language Communication for Hearing Impaired:
    Effective communication is essential for hearing impaired students to participate fully in educational and social activities. However, existing sign language communication tools often face challenges in providing accurate and real-time translations between sign language and spoken or written language.

  3. Object Detection with Facial Expression Recognition for Visually Impaired:
    Visually impaired students encounter unique obstacles in navigating their surroundings and interpreting social cues. Integrating object detection with facial expression recognition technologies can enhance their independence and interaction capabilities significantly. Current assistive technologies may struggle with real-time object recognition and facial expression interpretation, limiting their effectiveness.

  4. Sign Language Learning for Hearing Impaired:
    Learning sign language is fundamental for hearing impaired individuals to communicate effectively and engage fully in educational and social settings. However, existing sign language learning tools often lack personalized learning paths and comprehensive feedback mechanisms. Improving these technologies would provide tailored learning experiences.


Main Objective

Our main objective is to create an E-Learning platform that encourages communication by teaching sign language, Sign Language for communication, and visual content description while offering users emotional support.

  1. Analyse student emotions, detect stress levels, and provide personalized support. Gamification techniques are used to promote a positive learning environment.

  2. The sign language communication provides interpret hand movements of sign language users, translating gestures into text and spoken language.

  3. Combination of facial detection and emotional detection to identify objects in the surroundings and detect the emotions of the person.

  4. Sign language engine offers real-time feedback on sign language proficiency, revolutionizing sign language learning for better accessibility and effectiveness.



METHODOLOGY

The proposed real-time support and detection system consists of 4 main components. They are:

    1. Emotional Support and Stress Detection.
    2. Visual Content Description.
    3. Sign Language Communication.
    4. Sign Language Learning.

he overall system diagram of the proposed solution is intended to provide a smart, real-time approach for stakeholders, researchers, and caregivers to support hearing impaired and visually impaired students and individuals. As shown in the diagram, the registered users of the system can capture the surrounding in real-time. The data is sent to the Amazon Web Services (AWS) backend server where the Flask server is deployed. This data is processed in the Flask server by the designed DCNN models for emotional support and stress detection. If emotional distress is found, the severity will be determined using CNN models.

Simultaneously, if object detection is required, the data will go through the YOLOv8 object detection algorithm to assist visually impaired students in recognizing and navigating around objects. Data will be classified using the CNN models for sign language communication and sign language learning at the same time. The sign language communication component is responsible for real-time translation between sign language and spoken or written language, while the sign language learning component will check the signs and provide assessments for learning purposes.
